Benefits of Quitting

  • 20 minutes after quitting, blood pressure and heart rate drops to a normal rate and the temperature of extremities increases to normal. This decreases stress on the body.
  • Carbon monoxide levels in the blood decrease
  • Decreased risk of heart attack, stroke, lung cancer, and other tobacco-related illnesses
  • Increased circulation and lung capacity
  • Coughing, sinus congestion, fatigue, and shortness of breath decrease. Your body is better able to deal with mucus formed naturally.
  • By quitting, you can save money that you would have otherwise spent on tobacco
  • Decrease in the cost of healthcare to treat tobacco-related illnesses
  • Your looks will improve as your body has fewer toxins and your teeth and skin are not stained by tobacco
  • Improved sense of taste and smell

Tips to Quit

  • Break the triggers: If every day after morning coffee you light a cigarette, then just stop the habit of smoking after coffee. You may smoke later, but not directly after the coffee. Just break the autopilot habits. Don't let a pattern be formed.
  • Never keep a pack of cigarettes or tobacco with you.
  • If you have friend who uses tobacco and if he is not ready to quit then find a new friend.
  • Find ways to grow a dislike towards tobacco. Learn about people who used tobacco, and associate tobacco with pain, not pleasure.
  • When you smoke, think that with every drag how much soot you are collecting in your lungs. When you chew tobacco, think you may never be able to eat anything else forever.
